How to Reach

By Air

Indira Gandhi International Airport (DEL) is the nearest airport to the conference venue. It is approximately 25 km from the venue and serves as a major international gateway to India with connections to most major cities worldwide.

Transportation from the airport:

  • Airport Express Metro: The fastest way to reach central Delhi. Get off at New Delhi Metro Station and then take a taxi or regular metro to reach the venue.
  • Taxis: Pre-paid taxis are available at the airport. The fare to Delhi University area is approximately INR 600-800.
  • App-based Cabs: Uber and Ola services are readily available from the airport.
By Metro

Delhi has an excellent metro system connecting most parts of the city.

  • Nearest Metro Station: Vishwavidyalaya (Yellow Line) - approximately 2 km from the venue
  • From the metro station, you can take an auto-rickshaw or a taxi to reach the venue.
By Train

Delhi is well-connected by trains from all major cities in India. The city has several railway stations, with New Delhi Railway Station and Delhi Junction (Old Delhi) being the major ones.

  • From New Delhi Railway Station, you can take the Yellow Line metro to Vishwavidyalaya Station.
  • From Delhi Junction, you can take an auto-rickshaw or taxi to the venue.
Local Transportation

Delhi offers various options for local transportation:

  • Metro: The most convenient way to travel around Delhi.
  • App-based Cabs: Uber and Ola are widely available.
  • Auto-rickshaws: Economical option for short distances.
  • Cycle-rickshaws: Available for very short distances in certain areas.

Tip: Download the Delhi Metro app to navigate the metro system easily.

About Delhi

Climate

The climate of Delhi in the month of September is normally pleasant with temperatures ranging from 15 to 30 degrees Celsius. The monsoon season begins to recede by late September, making it a comfortable time to visit.

Tourism

Delhi is a city with rich historical significance and cultural heritage. Some of the famous tourist attractions include:

  • Red Fort
  • Qutub Minar
  • India Gate
  • Humayun's Tomb
  • Lotus Temple
  • Akshardham Temple
  • Chandni Chowk Market
Post-Conference Sightseeing

A sightseeing tour to some of the tourist attractions in Delhi will be arranged separately on September 22, 2025. The participants will be asked to bear the cost of the tour depending on the distance, duration of the trip, and the number of delegates.

Those interested may contact the organizing committee at collnet2025@gmail.com.

Exploring Beyond Delhi

Delhi is also an excellent base for exploring other parts of Northern India:

  • Agra (Taj Mahal) - Approximately 230 km from Delhi
  • Jaipur - Approximately 280 km from Delhi
  • Rishikesh - Approximately 240 km from Delhi

These destinations can be reached by train, bus, or through organized tours.

Visa Information

Most foreign nationals require a visa to enter India. We recommend applying for your visa well in advance of your travel date.

e-Visa Facility

India offers an e-Visa facility for nationals of many countries. The e-Visa can be applied for online and generally takes 3-5 business days to process.

For more information and to apply for an e-Visa, please visit the official Indian e-Visa website: https://indianvisaonline.gov.in/

Invitation Letter

If you require an invitation letter for visa purposes, please send your request to collnet2025@gmail.com with the subject line "Visa Invitation Letter Request". Include your full name, passport number, and country of citizenship in the email.

Note: The organizing committee will provide assistance for visa-related queries, but it is the responsibility of the participant to apply for and secure the appropriate visa.
