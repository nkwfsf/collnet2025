COLLNET 2025

A very warm welcome to the 19th International Conference on Webometrics, Informetrics and Scientometrics (WIS) and the 24th COLLNET Meeting, 2025 which is scheduled to held from 19-21 September 2025 at Institute of Economic Growth (University of Delhi Enclave, New Delhi, India)  organized by COLLNET in association with Society for Library Professionals (SLP) and Asia Community, Special Libraries Association (SLA).

COLLNET has a journey of glorious twenty-two years along with organizing the International Conferences on Webometrics, Informetrics and Scientometrics (WIS), held  in Berlin (Germany) in 2000, New Delhi (India) in 2001, Sydney (Australia) in 2002, Beijing (China) in 2003, Roorkee (India) in 2004, Stockholm (Sweden) in 2005, Nancy (France) in 2006, New Delhi (India) in 2007, Berlin (Germany) in 2008, Dalian (China) in 2009, Mysore (India) in 2010, Istanbul (Turkey) in 2011, Seoul (Korea) in 2012, Tartu (Estonia) in 2013, Ilmenau (Germany) in 2014, New Delhi (India) in 2015, Nancy (France) in 2016, Canterbury (UK) in 2017, Macau in 2018, Dalian (China) in 2019, Bangkok (Thailand) in 2022, 2023 in Dhaka (Bangladesh) and 2024 in Strasburg, France.

COLLNET 2025 will provide a unique platform for Scientists, Information Professionals, Librarians, Researchers and Practitioners to share experiences, ideas, research results, meeting and networking on all aspects of Webometrics, Informetrics and Scientometrics. Society for Library Professionals (SLP)

The Society for Library Professionals (SLP) was founded in the year 2008 under the Societies Registration Act 1860. The role of this Society is to achieve high standards in the field of librarianship. This will be achieved by the development of strong linkages among libraries and information centers across the globe; advancement of library and information science profession in India by fostering collaboration with national and international agencies; and also by promoting educational exchange programs with global partners. For further details visit http://www.slp.org.in

Scope

The broad aim of the conference is collaboration and communication in science and technology; science policy; quantitative aspects of science of science; and combination and integration of qualitative and quantitative approaches in study of scientific practices. The conference strives to contribute evidence-based and informed knowledge about scientific research and practices which may then be used to drive the formulation of institutional, regional, national, and worldwide research and innovation policy.

Some of the major objectives are to:

  • Ensure that information professionals are acquainted with the latest developments in the library and information world.
  • Bring together the dispersed community of library professionals to share their experiences through a common platform.
  • Assist libraries and library professionals in implementing their development plans.
  • Learn innovative library tools and technologies used to create and deliver better library support services.
  • Strengthen academic collaboration and strategic alliances for the development of special libraries.

Thematic Areas

The conference will cover theoretical, methodological and applied topics in academia which will comprise the following issues, but not necessarily limited to:

  • Bibliometrics, Scientometrics, Webometrics/ Cybermetrics, Informetrics and Altmetrics
  • Snowball Metrics
  • Citation Indexing Services
  • Research Metrics and Analytics for Scholarly Communication
  • Implementing Fair and Responsible Methods
  • Quantitative Analysis of S&T Innovations
  • Collaboration in Science and Technology from both Quantitative and Qualitative Points of View
  • Academic-Corporate Collaboration and Impact
  • Data Science, Big Data and Big Data Analytics
  • Information and Knowledge Management
  • Technology & Innovations in Libraries and their Impact on Measurement on Learning, Research and Users
  • AI Impact on Science of Science
  • Education for Applied Information Science
  • Emerging Technologies in Library and Information Science
  • Sustainable Science and Technology Metrics

Paper Submission Guidelines

Participants are invited to contribute papers for presentation on the above themes and other related aspects. The papers should be based on research surveys; case studies or action plans rather than theoretical explanations. Papers should be sent to collnet2025@gmail.com

Extended abstract of two pages containing gist of the paper enumerating the objective, methodology etc. in about 500 words is to be sent within the stipulated date. Submission of full papers with an abstract in approximately 200 words, keywords and references on A4 size paper with margin 1.5 cm on all side in MS Word format in 12 points New Times Roman type single line spacing. Length of the paper should not exceed 3000 words. Details such as name of the author, designation, title of the article, abstract, name of the affiliating institution, mailing address, telephone number, fax number and E-mail address are to be included. The contributors will get intimation regarding the acceptance of the paper by the Program Committee. The paper submitted for presentation should not have already been published/presented elsewhere. The Program Committee will have the right to edit the papers. Selected papers will be published in the proceedings. References of the paper must be prepared according to the guideline of APA Referencing Style.
